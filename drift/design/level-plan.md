# Drift Level Plan

## Level Design Rules

- Every level should have corners that reward hop-to-drift.
- Straights should be short enough that the player returns to corner decisions quickly.
- Checkpoints should be generous but ordered, so players cannot skip the intended route.
- Track boundaries should recover the player quickly rather than causing long failures.
- Medal targets should be tuned only after the car feel is stable.

## Level 1: Harbor Hairpins

Purpose: teach the core mechanic.

- Compact loop.
- Three 90-degree turns.
- One wide 180-degree hairpin.
- Short straights between corners.
- Low walls and clear checkpoint gates.

Primary lesson: tap `Space`, hold through landing, slide around the corner, release to regain grip.

## Level 2: Skyline Sprint

Purpose: reward speed management.

- Point-to-point course.
- Longer sweepers.
- One jump-like crest that should not require platforming precision.
- Wider roads and higher top speed.

Primary lesson: choose which turns deserve a drift and which should be taken with normal grip.

## Level 3: Canyon Switchbacks

Purpose: link drifts.

- Downhill switchback layout.
- Back-to-back turns in alternating directions.
- Slight elevation changes.
- Higher risk from entering a drift too late.

Primary lesson: exit one drift in a good position to set up the next hop.

## Future Level Ideas

- `Neon Overpass`: city course with traffic barriers and split routes.
- `Snowline Pass`: lower grip level where drift timing changes.
- `Foundry Run`: industrial course with moving doors or timed hazards.

## Medal Model

Each level should define:

- Bronze target: clean finish with several mistakes.
- Silver target: understands most drift opportunities.
- Gold target: clean drift lines and fast restarts.
- Platinum target: near-optimized route for leaderboard chasing.
